John (Jack) Tieszen

MLIS Candidate at the University of California, Los Angeles.

Hello, my name is John (though I prefer Jack) Roblee Tieszen and I am an upcoming graduate from the University of California, Los Angeles currently in the process of receiving my MLIS. I was born in the state of Minnesota and continued my tour of the midwest by receiving a bachelor’s degree in cinema studies and geography at the University of Iowa. I also received a minor in informatics as well from the same university. I decided to take my polymathic interests and apply it which is what led me to archiving and librarianship. My main interests lie in academic institutions, more specifically in special collections but I have done much work in outreach, visualization, reference desk, and other forms of librarianship for both public and private institutions. My research focuses are in accessibility and how the construction of space can lead to student anxiety, LGBTQ+ library initiatives, Mental health, How the library plays a role in student life, Digital shifts in librarianship, and Storytelling as a useful tool for library’s and archives.
